Where to Buy Waveplates

Waveplates are transparent plates with a defined amount of birefringence, used for modifying the polarization of light. Most common are quarter waveplates (λ / 4 plates) and half waveplates (λ / 2 plates). One may, for example, use a half waveplate for rotating a linear polarization state, or a quarter waveplate for producing circularly polarized light.

ESee also our encyclopedia article on waveplates!
Related articles: polarization of laser emission, fiber polarization controllers, Babinet–Soleil compensators, polarizers

Related products: Babinet–Soleil compensators

Related product category: optical components and devices

Check List for Waveplates

Click here to see a check list before buying such products!
  • What retardance do you need – λ/2, λ/4 or anything else, for what wavelength(s)?
  • Will a simple multi-order waveplate be sufficient, or do you need a (true or effective) zero-order waveplate, which can be operated in a much wider wavelength range? (There are also achromatic waveplates for particularly wide wavelength regions.)
  • Should it have anti-reflection coatings – for what wavelengths?
  • Is the surface quality high enough?
  • For use with pulsed lasers: is the optical damage threshold sufficiently high?
  • Might operation in a wide temperature range be a problem?

In case that you need additional advice, consultancy from RP Photonics may be the solution.
