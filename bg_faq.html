Can I trust buyer's guides?

As a user, you generally can trust that the common buyer's guides don't suppress or prevent important suppliers from being listed. However, they may present companies for particular products when in fact they cannot deliver that. While not a risk, such poor data quality is a nuisance. The risk of overlooking important suppliers is much greater if you rely on internet searches.

As an advertiser, you should be very careful. For example, many published traffic statistics for websites are highly misleading, and some are even completely wrong – often inflating the data. Our recommendations include:

  • Treating all published traffic data with great care. Ask what exactly they apply to, for example the buyer's guide pages or the whole website, which also contain thousands of articles which are irrelevant for your purposes. Check plausibility of the data by investigating the search engine ranking (at least with a quick search for some important keywords).
  • Check yourself what is most relevant for you: the incoming traffic to your web server. You should at least study statistics on how many visits you obtain from different web domains. If possible, compare that with what they claim and draw your own conclusions.

We emphasize that our adverting offers are thoroughly honest and trustworthy.

How can I objectively compare online advertising resources?

We warmly recommend that you check your referral traffic. This is a good indication for how much marketing value is delivered by different websites. It depends on how many professionals actually use the sites and their features.

Of course, not everything of importance can be quantified. However, the nicest website can't deliver quality marketing if it cannot generate substantial referrals traffic.
